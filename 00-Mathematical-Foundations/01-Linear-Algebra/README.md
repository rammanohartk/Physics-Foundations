# Linear Algebra for Physics

Linear algebra provides the mathematical framework for describing and analyzing physical systems in a structured and coordinate-independent way. It forms the foundation for understanding vectors, transformations, and the behavior of complex systems.

In physics, many problems reduce to understanding how quantities evolve under linear operations. Vectors represent the state of a system, while matrices act as operators that transform these states. Eigenvalues and eigenvectors reveal the natural modes of a system, where complex interactions simplify into independent behavior.

A key idea is that the same physical system can appear complicated or simple depending on the choice of basis. By choosing an appropriate basis—often the eigenbasis—coupled systems can be decoupled, and underlying structure becomes clear.

From a computational perspective, linear algebra is central to numerical methods. Many problems in simulation and modeling reduce to matrix equations, eigenvalue problems, and vector operations. Techniques such as discretization transform continuous physical systems into linear algebra problems that can be solved efficiently on a computer.

This section develops the essential concepts of linear algebra with an emphasis on physical interpretation and computational relevance.
